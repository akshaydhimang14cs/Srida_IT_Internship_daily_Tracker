# Module 2: Compliance Audit Essentials

## Overview
This module introduces compliance auditing and the structured methods used to plan, scope, manage, and report audits. It shows how audits support governance, risk, and compliance by verifying whether controls, policies, and standards are followed in practice.

## Duration Covered
08 June 2026 to 11 June 2026

## Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction to Compliance Auditing
Compliance auditing is a systematic, independent, and documented process for evaluating evidence against criteria. The module explains audit types, the difference between audits, assessments, and reviews, ISO 19011:2018 principles, the audit lifecycle, and why auditing is essential in GRC.

### 2. Audit Planning and Scope Definition
This section explains how to define audit objectives, criteria, scope boundaries, exclusions, and approvals before fieldwork begins. It stresses that clear scope prevents scope creep and keeps the audit focused on the correct processes, systems, locations, and time period.

### 3. Audit Plan Components and Resource Requirements
The module covers the audit plan as the roadmap for engagement execution. It includes objectives, scope, criteria, methods, roles, schedule, logistics, communication, and confidentiality, while also highlighting resource planning for people, tools, access, and budget.

### 4. Risk-Based Audit Planning
Risk-based planning increases audit depth in higher-risk areas and relies on prior audit results, incidents, risk registers, regulatory changes, and continuous monitoring. A pre-audit checklist ensures preparation is complete before the audit starts.

### 5. Building the Audit Program
An audit program is the strategic framework for multiple audits across a year. The module explains annual program design, program objectives, risk-based prioritization, mandatory audits, KPIs, resources, and contingency planning.

### 6. Auditor Competency and Team Management
Auditor quality depends on technical and behavioural competencies, independence, CPD, and effective team composition. The module also explains competency matrices, cooling-off periods, and the roles of lead auditors, team auditors, experts, and observers.

### 7. Annual Scheduling and Audit Frequency
Audit schedules should be published early and based on risk level, control maturity, prior findings, regulatory obligations, and organizational cycles. High-risk areas need more frequent audits, while stable low-risk areas may be audited less often.

### 8. Audit Evidence and Sampling
The module identifies documentary, testimonial, observational, analytical, and digital evidence, then explains sufficiency, reliability, relevance, chain of custody, and sampling methods such as statistical, non-statistical, judgmental, and attribute sampling.

### 9. Interviewing, Observation, and Walkthroughs
Interview and observation methods help validate how controls work in practice. The module recommends open, closed, probing, hypothetical, and clarifying questions, plus walkthroughs and strong note-taking to compare documented processes against real execution.

### 10. ISO 27001 Compliance Auditing
ISO 27001 is presented as the ISMS framework built on clauses 4 to 10 and mandatory documentation such as scope, policy, risk assessment, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, objectives, competence evidence, internal audit results, management review minutes, and corrective actions.

### 11. Stage 1 and Stage 2 Audit Approach
Stage 1 checks readiness and documentation, while Stage 2 checks implementation and operating effectiveness. The module explains the importance of the Statement of Applicability, Annex A control coverage, and evidence-based verification.

### 12. Clause-by-Clause ISO 27001 Review
The module details Clauses 4 to 10 and the common findings linked to each clause. It shows how context, leadership, planning, support, operation, performance evaluation, and improvement are evaluated during an ISMS audit.

### 13. SOC 2 Readiness and Trust Services Criteria
SOC 2 readiness covers Security as mandatory and the other Trust Services Criteria as applicable. The module explains Type I versus Type II reporting, design effectiveness versus operating effectiveness, and how readiness assessments and gap analysis support exam preparation.

### 14. SOC 2 Readiness Roadmap
The readiness roadmap includes assessment, remediation, operating period, pre-audit readiness, and examination. The module also describes selecting a CPA firm, management assertions, and inclusive or carve-out treatment of sub-service organizations.

### 15. GDPR and Privacy Auditing
Privacy audits focus on lawful collection, processing, storage, sharing, and deletion of personal data. The module explains GDPR scope, records of processing, legal basis, consent, data subject rights, DPO role, transfers, breach notification, and DPIAs.

### 16. Privacy Governance and Vendor Oversight
This section explains privacy by design, privacy by default, and the need to build privacy controls into systems, SDLC, change management, and vendor assessments. It also highlights the importance of DPAs and vendor oversight in privacy compliance.

### 17. Audit Reporting and Finding Classification
The module classifies findings as major nonconformity, minor nonconformity, observation, or opportunity for improvement. It emphasizes evidence-based reporting because finding severity affects certification, timelines, and response requirements.

### 18. Audit Report Structure and the 4 Cs
The report should include the executive summary, scope, methodology, findings, conclusions, recommendations, and appendices. The module uses the 4 Cs framework—Condition, Criteria, Cause, and Effect—to make findings clear and defensible.

### 19. Root Cause Analysis and Corrective Action
The module introduces root cause analysis tools such as 5 Whys, Fishbone diagrams, and Fault Tree Analysis. It then connects them to corrective action management, CAPA tracking, verification, and closure requirements.

### 20. Report Writing Best Practices
Objective language, specific evidence references, and quantification of impact strengthen audit reports. The module advises avoiding blame-oriented wording and using numbers, dates, and affected populations to show risk clearly.

### 21. Appendix and Report Planning
The module provides a report content model showing the likely length and purpose of the cover page, executive summary, audit scope, methodology, findings, conclusions, and appendices. This helps in structuring final audit reporting professionally.

### 22. Quick Revision
The module ends with a consolidated quick-revision view: compliance audits are evidence-based, scope and planning matter, programs require governance, evidence and sampling drive reliability, ISO 27001 and SOC 2 need structured evaluation, privacy audits require lawful processing checks, and corrective action is necessary for closure.

## Overall Learning Outcome
This module built a strong foundation in compliance audit concepts, audit planning, audit program management, evidence collection, ISO 27001 auditing, SOC 2 readiness, GDPR and privacy compliance, and audit reporting. It shows how audits are structured, how scope is defined, how evidence is gathered and sampled, how findings are written, and how corrective actions are verified.
